Parts of a Cell

Nucleus Mitochondria Ribosomes Cytoplasm Cell Membrane

Nucleus

The Control Center

The nucleus is the control center of the cell, containing the cell's DNA. It regulates gene expression and cell division.

Mitochondria

Powerhouse of the Cell

Mitochondria generate energy for the cell by converting nutrients into ATP, which powers various cell processes.

Ribosomes

Protein Factories

Ribosomes are responsible for synthesizing proteins by translating mRNA into amino acid chains.

Cytoplasm

Cell's Environment

The cytoplasm is the jelly-like substance that fills the space between the cell membrane and the nucleus. It houses the organelles.

Cell Membrane

Protective Barrier

The cell membrane is a selective barrier that regulates what enters and exits the cell, maintaining the cell's internal environment.
